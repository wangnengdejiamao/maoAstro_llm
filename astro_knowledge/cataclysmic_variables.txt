Cataclysmic Variables (CVs) - Comprehensive Guide
=================================================

1. SYSTEM COMPOSITION
   - Primary: White dwarf (mass 0.6-1.2 M_sun)
   - Secondary: Low-mass star (usually M dwarf, mass < 1 M_sun)
   - Orbital period: Typically < 1 day (1-12 hours range)
   - Separation: Very close (solar radius scale)

2. MASS TRANSFER MECHANISMS
   - Roche lobe overflow: Secondary star fills its Roche lobe
   - Angular momentum loss:
     * Magnetic braking (for periods > 2-3 hours)
     * Gravitational wave radiation (for periods < 2 hours)
   - Mass transfer rate: 10^-11 to 10^-8 M_sun/year

3. CV TYPES AND CLASSIFICATION

   3.1 Non-Magnetic CVs (B < 1 MG)
   - Dwarf Novae (DN):
     * U Gem type: Regular outbursts, 2-6 mag amplitude
     * SU UMa type: Superoutbursts + normal outbursts, superhumps
     * Z Cam type: Standstill periods between outbursts
   - Nova-like Variables (NL):
     * UX UMa type: High mass transfer, always bright
     * VY Scl type: Occasional low states
   - Classical Novae: Thermonuclear runaways (10^4-10^5 year intervals)
   - Recurrent Novae: Multiple nova eruptions observed

   3.2 Magnetic CVs
   - Polars (AM Her type, B > 10 MG):
     * Synchronous rotation (P_spin = P_orb)
     * No accretion disk
     * Strong polarization (up to 30%)
     * X-ray emission from accretion column
   - Intermediate Polars (DQ Her type, 1 < B < 10 MG):
     * Non-synchronous rotation
     * Partial accretion disk or disk truncated by magnetosphere
     * Spin period shorter than orbital period

4. ACCRETION DISK PHYSICS
   - Standard disk: Gas orbits white dwarf, viscous heating
   - Boundary layer: Where disk meets WD surface, hot (10^7-10^8 K)
   - Hot spot: Where stream hits disk
   - Disk instability model (DIM): Explains dwarf nova outbursts

5. OBSERVATIONAL PROPERTIES

   5.1 Photometric
   - Outbursts: 2-6 mag for dwarf novae, 6-19 mag for novae
   - Quiescence: Faint, red colors
   - Flickering: Short timescale variability (minutes)
   - Orbital modulation: From hot spot or ellipsoidal effect

   5.2 Spectroscopic
   - Emission lines: H I, He I, He II (strongest in quiescence)
   - Double-peaked profiles: From rotating accretion disk
   - Absorption lines: From secondary star (often hard to detect)
   - Bowen blend: N III/C III at ~4640 Å (indicator of high excitation)

6. PERIOD GAP AND EVOLUTION
   - Period gap: 2-3 hour range with few systems
   - Below gap: GW-driven evolution
   - Above gap: Magnetic braking drives evolution
   - Minimum period: ~70 minutes (period bounce)

7. DISTINGUISHING FEATURES FROM OTHER SYSTEMS
   - AM CVn: No hydrogen, He-rich, P < 65 min
   - Symbiotic stars: Giant donor, longer P
   - X-ray binaries: Neutron star or black hole primary
   - Algol systems: Non-degenerate primary

8. IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
   - Do NOT confuse with AM CVn systems (which are He-transferring)
   - Light variations are from accretion processes, NOT tidal interactions
   - Period-luminosity relations are different from pulsating variables
